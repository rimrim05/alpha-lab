# Vol-managed levered QQQ

Realized vol clusters but does not forecast returns, so return-per-unit-risk is highest when vol is low. Each close we size QQQ at sigma_target / realized_vol(21d), capped at 2x, with a 0.05 no-trade band to keep turnover near zero. The other side is leverage-constrained, drawdown-averse investors (and vol-target mandates that de-risk after spikes, at the lows) who will not hold 1.5-2x in calm markets and sell into high-vol episodes; the strategy harvests the equity premium at near-constant risk, levered in calm regimes, mechanically small through crashes (Moreira & Muir, JF 2017). Honest caveat: most of the return is levered QQQ beta; the vol timing adds ~2-4 CAGR points and ~0.15 Sharpe. Falsifier: a forward year where the vol-scaled version underperforms constant-leverage QQQ risk-adjusted (i.e. low-vol regimes stop carrying higher return-per-risk), or a flat/down tech year: it will not clear +18% unless QQQ returns > ~10-12%.
