# vol_core_svxy — mechanism

Two premia with the same insurance buyer on the other side. The core (60/40 QQQ/SPY, each leg scaled to a 25% vol target off 21-day realized vol, 2x leverage cap) harvests the equity risk premium at constant risk: leverage rises in calm markets where realized returns per unit of vol are historically better, and de-levers into vol spikes, which mechanically cuts drawdowns without forecasting returns. The sleeve holds a fixed 0.3 in SVXY only when VIX closes below its own 63-day rolling median: the VIX futures curve sits in contango most of the time because institutions systematically overpay for crash protection (Eraker & Wu 2017), and short-VIX collects that premium, but its fatal mode (short vol into a spike) lives in elevated-VIX regimes, which the gate excludes (it sat out Feb-2018 and Mar-2020 on train). Falsifiers forward: (1) a sustained bear or high-vol regime where the vol-managed core underperforms its unmanaged version after costs, i.e. the vol-timing premium disappears; (2) the VIX curve spending a low-VIX period in backwardation (or an SVXY spike loss occurring while VIX is below its rolling median), which would break the gate's premise that calm = contango; (3) turnover/cost drag persistently exceeding the sleeve's carry.
