# deep_dip_reversion — mechanism

Large-cap deep dips (60d sector-residual s-score ≤ −2.5) are dominated by forced and discretionary sellers completing liquidations, not by information; the lab's own statarb post-mortem showed ~43% of the neutral book's gross return lived in the −2σ to −3σ band and slow-reverting entries earned ~2x fast ones per trade. The edge persists at deep thresholds because such dips are rare: too infrequent to support the turnover-heavy industry version of the trade, so cheap-to-trade patient capital keeps it. Expressing it long-only (equal 10% slots up to 15 names at 1.5x gross, idle capital in SPY) converts the unhedgeable drift-drag that killed the market-neutral book into levered market beta (~1.8 effective) plus a reversion kicker, at ~1-2%/day one-way turnover instead of 5%+. Falsifier forward: if the entered names' post-entry excess return over their sector ETF is ≤ 0 over the holding period (i.e. the book does no better than 1.5x SPY net of the dip sleeve's costs) for a full year, the kicker is dead and this is just expensive levered beta: kill it. A deep down-market year is the known tail: losses concentrate at ~1.8 effective beta in exactly the names being liquidated.
