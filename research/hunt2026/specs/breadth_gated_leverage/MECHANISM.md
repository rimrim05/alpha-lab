# Breadth-gated leverage

When the equal-weight S&P (RSP) has outrun the cap-weight index (SPY) over the past
quarter (63d), the rally has broad participation; such regimes historically show higher
forward Sharpe and fewer crashes because narrow mega-cap-only rallies are late-cycle.
The strategy holds 2x SPY when 63d RSP/SPY relative strength is positive, and otherwise
de-levers SPY to a 15% vol target (21d realized vol, capped at 2x). The other side is
benchmark-hugging investors holding constant 1x exposure regardless of market internals;
the edge persists because leverage-switching on public breadth data requires conviction
and whipsaw tolerance. Known fragility: only the 63d lookback clears 18% CAGR on train
(neighbors ~17%), and 2022-2025H1 delivered just 9%/yr because mega-cap-concentrated
rallies keep it de-levered. **Falsifier:** a forward year where breadth-positive regimes
underperform breadth-negative ones on SPY (the gate inverts), or where the strategy lags
1x SPY by more than its de-lever drag would explain, i.e., narrow rallies continuing to
be the dominant, durable bull-market mode.
