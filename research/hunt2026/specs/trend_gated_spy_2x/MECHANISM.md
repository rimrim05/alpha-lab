# trend_gated_spy_2x

Volatility clusters and the worst equity drawdowns concentrate below long-term trend; above trend, realized vol is lower and returns higher, so daily 2x SPY exposure is applied only in the regime where variance drag is small and crash risk lowest (Gayed & Bilello, "Leverage for the Long Run", SSRN 2741701, 2016 Dow Award). Signal: SPY close vs its 200-day SMA with a 1% hysteresis band (switch only on a >1% cross, hold state inside the band) to cut whipsaw; risk-off leg is 1x BIL (T-bill carry, no SHY duration risk). The other side is every allocator whose mandate, margin, or loss aversion forces slow de-risking after drawdowns and late re-risking: a risk-transfer premium, not a mispricing, which is why a decade of full publicity has not degraded the 200DMA gate. Falsifier: forward evidence that below-trend regimes no longer carry elevated vol/crash concentration, i.e. the gated 2x persistently underperforms constant 2x SPY on a risk-adjusted basis across a full up-down cycle, or switch counts blow past ~10/yr with the band on (regime signal turned to noise).
